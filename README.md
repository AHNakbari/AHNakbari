## About

I'm a machine learning engineer working at the intersection of **applied LLMs, information retrieval, and knowledge representation** — the systems that turn messy, unstructured data into structured knowledge that both people and AI agents can actually use.

I'm drawn to problems that live between research and production: designing retrieval and RAG pipelines, modeling entities and relationships as graphs, and making the whole thing measurable, reliable, and fast at scale. My background spans transformer architectures — from GPT-style models built from scratch to time-series and diffusion models — alongside hands-on retrieval and graph-based matching systems.

## What I focus on

- **Retrieval & RAG** — embedding- and LLM-based retrieval, semantic search, ranking, and context construction for language models.
- **Knowledge graphs & entity resolution** — extracting entities, relationships, and facts; deduplication, contradiction detection, and handling temporal change.
- **Evaluation & reliability** — golden query sets, regression tests, source attribution, and diagnosing retrieval failures and hallucinations.
- **Production ML engineering** — maintainable Python pipelines and services, large-scale document processing, and deliberate trade-offs across cost, speed, and quality.
